A productive week with a couple of things worth calling out.

The biggest news is a brand-new repo from Canvas Computing: [`prompting`](https://github.com/canvascomputing/prompting), published today. It's a prompting guide grounded in actual research — not just vibes — which is a refreshing angle. Already picked up a few stars on day one, which is a good sign. I'll be leaning on this myself as the AI tooling work at Canvas continues to evolve.

On the personal projects side, [`LionPDF`](https://github.com/leochilds/LionPDF) got some attention this week. It's the privacy-first desktop PDF reader built with Tauri, Rust, and React — one of those projects I keep coming back to whenever I want to go deep on Rust without the pressure of a deadline. There's something satisfying about building native desktop software in 2026.

[`agentwerk`](https://github.com/canvascomputing/agentwerk) also had commits land this week, continuing its steady evolution since the rename from `agent`. And over at [`Kyri-Cou/cryptography`](https://github.com/Kyri-Cou/cryptography), the interactive companion site for the YouTube course is still getting regular updates — new ciphers and tools are clearly in the works.

Quiet but steady progress overall. The `malwi` ecosystem is really starting to feel like a proper suite of tools now — `malwi`, `malwi-benchmark`, and `malwi-samples` all sitting alongside each other.
